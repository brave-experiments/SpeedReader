Accused Houston serial killer could face the death penalty

By Jay R. Jordan

Published 6:45 am CST, Thursday, February 20, 2020

    Lucky Ward, 55, is considered Harris County’s “most dangerous inmate”

    Photo: Melissa Phillip/Houston Chronicle

There’s extra security in a downtown Houston courtroom where a man is on trial over the strangling of two women – even though he’s been suspected in many more similar deaths.

Lucky Ward, 55, is accused of killing Reita Long and Carlos Rodriguez, who went by “Gypsy” and “Charlie,” nearly 10 years ago. Both women were found within weeks of each other in September 2010 – one dead on the steps of the Co-Cathedral of the Sacred Heart with a bra wrapped around her neck and the other covered in blankets in a north Houston home.

Ward is also believed to have killed Myra Ical, 51; Raquel A. Mundy, 24; Carol E. Flood, 62. He was also tied to the 1985 slaying of Birdell Louis, another Houston woman who was strangled.
