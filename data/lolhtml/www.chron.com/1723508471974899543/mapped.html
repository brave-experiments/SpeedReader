A Labor Day weekend getaway doesn't have to include airfare and costly accommodations. We brainstormed short trips and did the math to see where a family of four (two adults and two children ages 9-10) can go and what they can do around Texas for under $500, not including meals.

Read about some of the stops below and get more details on the locations you can visit in the gallery above. 

Long weekend in Seguin:
Unpack and unwind at Son's Island, a family friendly resort where guests can enjoy water sports like paddle boarding and kayaking, fishing, swimming and camping.  There are also on-land activities, including volleyball, grilling and relaxing at a waterfront cabana. What all of that means: this resort is the only place you'll need to drive for a full, fun weekend. Patrons can choose between a day-long cabana rental for $99 per day or overnight camping tents (which sleep 4) for $89 per night. Approximate total, not including meals: $300. 

Two-night stay in Dallas:
Enjoy a picnic at the sprawling arboretum and then head to Dallas' impressive aquarium. Find an educational experience at the Sixth Floor Museum on the sixth floor of the Dallas County Administration Building. The place is dedicated to the history behind the assassination of President John F. Kennedy. Save money on lodging by booking a two-night stay in a central location through Airbnb. Approximate total (gas included): $414 plus food

One-day trip to New Braunfels:
Secure a one-day pass for the family at Schlitterbahn New Braunfels Waterpark, one of the state's premier summer attractions. The next morning, browse for countrified home goods and gifts at the Red Stag in New Braunfels' Design District. Visit the Animal World & Snake Farm Zoo, an animal lovers' post that inspired a Ray Wylie Hubbard song. On the way home, drive up to The Stars & Stripes Drive-In Theatre for a movie night. Approximate total (gas included): $398.23 plus food

Two-night tour around San Antonio:
On the drive into town, stop by the Mission San Juan Capistrano, a mission founded in 1731 by Franciscan Spanish priests. Take a walking tour through the wooded area behind the main gate, step into its historical exhibit to learn how the area's first settlers lived and then light a candle at the chapel. In town, take a boat tour down the River Walk and enjoy waterfall sounds at the Japanese Tea Garden before settling in for the night. The next day, head to the Alamo for an immersive educational experience before walking to Ripley's Believe It or Not. Approximate total (gas included): $350 plus food

Two-night stay in Fredericksburg:

Settled by Germans and founded in 1846, this Hill Country town has a number of educational activities for families. Visit the Historical Society Pioneer Museum to learn how early Texas settlers lived and then check out the Texas Rangers Heritage Center. Browse the quaint, well-preserved stores in Fredericksburg's Main Street shopping district before heading to the Auslander Restaurant and Biergarten for live music. Enjoy a unique lodging option at the Fredericksburg Herb Farm, where families can rent out a silo room. Approximate total (gas included): $440  plus food

All estimated costs can vary, including parking prices. Some costs don't include tax. Estimated gas costs (added in the approximate total) are based on a standard sedan using regular gas with Houston as the point of origin, via GasBuddy.com's Trip Cost Calculator.
