A dozen coaches who grinded on the recruiting trail in 2020

BySteve Wiltfong 22 hours ago

Ohio State’s Brian Hartline edged Clemson’s Todd Bates for 247Sports National Recruiter of the Year Honors in this 2020 cycle, with Hartline leading the charge in inking a special receiver class while Bates doing the same regarding the Tigers defensive line haul.

Now 247Sports takes a look at a dozen other recruiters that made an impact from a pure volume standpoint, coaches that rolled up their sleeves in helping their respective programs secure the bulk of their classes.

Vince Marrow - Kentucky Wildcats

 

The Wildcats had the 247Sports Composite’s No. 23 recruiting class led by Vince Marrow’s efforts in helping sign 11 of the program’s 23 commits this past cycle.

When you look at the state that has been most important to Kentucky’s on-field success under head coach Mark Stoops, it’s been Ohio, where Marrow takes the lead and it was another good year for Big Blue Nation in the Buckeye State headlined by the No. 4 overall prospect in Top247 all-purpose back Michael Drennen II choosing to play for Kentucky over USC among many others. Other highly-recruited Ohio prospects to choose the Wildcats included running back Jutahn McClain, cornerback Carrington Valentine and receiver Kalil Branham.

Marrow took over the in-state efforts for the Wildcats late in the 2019 cycle and it paid off in 2020 with offensive lineman John Young, safety Vito Tisdale and defensive lineman Octavious Oxendine all choosing to Kentucky over strong offer lists. Young was the No. 1 player in Louisville.

Marrow, who serves as the associate head coach, tight ends and recruiting coordinator, also played a hand in securing transfer quarterback Joey Gatewood from Auburn.

Florida State has two grinders in David Johnson and Kenny Dillingham

 

Two of the hardest-working guys in the 2020 cycle are now on this brand new Seminoles staff in running backs coach/recruiting coordinator David Johnson and offensive coordinator Kenny Dillingham.

Johnson has 11 prospects listed to his name, and while the bulk of his work was done in Knoxville for Tennessee, he helped the Seminoles secure a late pledge from one of the fastest prospects in country in one-time Georgia commit Corey Wren and he also had the lead on athlete Darion Williamson and defensive end Markeston Douglas in choosing the Seminoles. Johnson was big for the Vols in Memphis which included Top247 defensive tackle Omari Thomas and four-star recruits Bryson Eason and Martavius French.

Meanwhile, Dillingham had nine recruits to his name which also included most of the cycle at Auburn, but he quickly made a splash at FSU flipping a pair of quarterbacks in four-star Chubba Purdy from Louisville and Tate Rodemaker from USF, and played a part in every offensive player signed down the stretch for Mike Norvell and company. For the Tigers he was big on the JUCO offensive tackle front going out West to land Kilian Zierer and Brenden Coffey.

Johnson and Dillingham reunite with Norvell after helping him build Memphis into one of the best Group of Five programs in America.

Darrin Chiaverini - Colorado Buffaloes

 

The new interim head coach for Colorado in Darrin Chiaverini loves his alma mater and it shows on the trail.

In 2020 Chiaverini was responsible for 10 more prospects putting pen to paper in favor of the Buffaloes including Brenden Rice choosing Boulder over Arizona State and Michigan among others, and flipping four-star safety Christian Gonzalez from Purdue.

Other big gets for Chiaverini included tight end Caleb Fauria who at one point was considered an Iowa lean, one of the best two-way players in California in Montana Lemonious-Craig and Lone Star State pass rusher Toren Pittman. He was the secondary on quarterback Brendon Lewis, a talented dual-threat signal-caller with a high ceiling as well.

Drop the interim tag and the Buffaloes would instantly have one of the hardest-working head coaches on the recruiting trail in the country who has strong ties in Texas and California.

Karl Scott - Alabama Crimson Tide

 

Alabama had the nation’s No. 2 recruiting class with secondary coach Karl Scott playing a big part in that with a couple massive Lone Star State flips at the expense of Oklahoma.

Scott is listed as the lead recruiter for five-star athlete Drew Sanders, the No. 1 prospect in Texas per the Top247, who besides his one-time verbal to the Sooners also strongly considered LSU and took officials to all three before opting for the Tide a few days after his trip to Tuscaloosa in April. He is also the lead for Top247 running back Jase McClellan who had been in the fold for Lincoln Riley and company since July 2017 but announced his flip to Alabama late in the process.

With 10 commits to his name overall, Scott finished as the top-ranked recruiter in the SEC and No. 3 overall in the 247Sports Recruiter Rankings which also included his efforts in signing Top247 outside linebacker Quandarrius Robinson, JUCO cornerback Ronald Williams and offensive tackle Damieon George.

Rodney Garner - Auburn Tigers

 

In signing nine prospects in the 2020 cycle, Auburn defensive line coach Rodney Garner finished No. 4 nationally in the 247Sports Recruiter Rankings and No. 2 in the SEC.

Among Garner’s best work this past class included a pair of defensive linemen pegged to go elsewhere nearly the whole cycle in Top247 defensive end Zykeivous Walker who he beat in-state Georgia on and Top247 defensive tackle Jay Hardy who he snagged out of Tennessee over the Vols. A couple of the nation’s top JUCO defensive linemen also chose to play at Auburn in Deandre Butler and Marquis Burks as Garner aims to reload quickly across his front, which was arguably the most talented in college football a season ago.

Auburn finished with the nation’s No. 7 recruiting class, the highest mark since 2014.

Antonio Pierce - Arizona State Sun Devils

 

The Sun Devils finished with the nation’s No. 24 recruiting class, a big reason why newly-minted Co-defensive coordinator Antonio Pierce, who also carries the Recruiting Coordinator title along with linebacker duties.

Pierce does a lot of his best work in the Los Angeles area and in 2020 that included securing a signature from Top247 linebacker Jordan Banks, flipping Top247 receiver Johnny Wilson from Oregon and winning a blue-chip battle for another Top247 receiver in LV Bunkley-Shelton late in the cycle. He was also the lead recruiter for four-star receiver Chad Johnson Jr.

This was Arizona State’s first Top25 class since the 2016 cycle. Pierce, a former Super Bowl champion and high school coach, can relate to kids in a variety of ways and should keep the Sun Devils in that mix for the years to come.

Pierce finished as the Pac 12’s No. 2 recruiter and No. 25 nationally.

Jay Bateman - North Carolina Tar Heels

North Carolina defensive coordinator Jay Bateman played part in the Tar Heels signing eight prospects en route to the 247Sports Composite’s No. 19 recruiting class, good for No. 3 in the ACC.

The defensive line haul coming to Chapel Hill was among the best penned in America, in defensive ends Desmond Evans, Myles Murphy and AJ Beatty and defensive tackles Kedrick Bingley-Jones and Clyde Pinder. However the star of the class could end up being safety Ja’Qurious Conley, who Bateman was listed as the primary for. Conley finished as the nation’s No. 94 prospect overall in the final Top247.

This past cycle was Bateman’s first Power Five opportunity after serving as the defensive coordinator for four years at Army and he put together a strong showing on the trail. Looking at the hot start in 2021, Bateman is a name we could see among the Top50 annually after checking in at No. 35 in 2020, good for No. 3 in the ACC.

Chris Partridge - Michigan Wolverines/Ole Miss Rebels

 

247Sports National Recruiter of the Year in 2017 Chris Partridge made a big impact for both programs he worked for in 2020.

Regarding a Wolverines class that finished No. 14 nationally and No. 2 in the Big Ten, Partridge spearheaded the recruitments for four-star safeties Jordan Morant and RJ Moten, and he helped discover Top247 tight end Matthew Hibner who ultimately finished as the No. 7 tight end in the Top247. Partridge also chipped in on flipping defensive end Aaron Lewis from West Virginia along with the signings of linebacker Nikhai Hill-Green and athlete William Mohan.

Upon arriving at Ole Miss, Partridge quickly led the charge in landing Top247 defensive end Demon Clowney, a former LSU commit, and also played a big part in landing transfer Otis Reese from Georgia because of his prior relationship in trying to recruit him to Michigan.

Partridge finished as the No. 37 recruiter in the country this past cycle.

Sean Spencer - Penn State Nittany Lions

 

Before taking a job with the New York Giants, former Nittany Lions defensive line coach Sean Spencer helped James Franklin and company sign a big class at the point of attack that included four-star defensive tackles in Coziah Izzard and Cole Brevard and four-star defensive end/outside linebacker Zuriah Fisher. Spencer finished as the No. 3 recruiter in the Big Ten and No. 22 nationally per 247Sports, and was not only known for his recruiting prowess, but for his eye for talent and player development.

In addition to being the defensive line coach, he is now the run-game coordinator for the Giants. Franklin hired John Scott Jr. away from South Carolina to replace Spencer.

Penn State signed the No. 15 class in the 2020 cycle which was good for No. 3 in the Big Ten.

Two more in the Big Ten

 

Nebraska running backs coach Ryan Held and Purdue co-offensive coordinator JaMarcus Shephard each helped their programs sign eight prospects apiece.

Held, who doubles as recruiting coordinator, was pivotal in a class that finished No. 20 nationally, good for No. 4 in the Big Ten, including a sneaky good running back class that included Sevion Morrison and Marvin Scott III. He also led the charge for Top247 offensive lineman Turner Corcoran and the top-ranked JUCO receiver in the land Omar Manning.

Purdue signed the nation’s No. 33 class highlighted by another strong group of pass catchers including Top247 tight end Maliq Carr and receiver Abdur-Rahmaan Yaseen who Shephard pulled from Michigan and speedy receiver Collin Sullivan out of the Lone Star State.

Held finished No. 6 in the Big Ten Recruiter Rankings while Shephard placed No. 9.
